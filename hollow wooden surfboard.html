About

This project was YEARS in the making and is a representation of my woodworking journey. I started with powertools and plywood and finished with hardwoods and hand tools. The board was designed using Shape3D and used to print out a template of the outline and support structure. The shape of the board is a 5'4" X 22.5" X 2.5" Mini Simmons with a diamond tail. The structure of this hollow board is called the "skeleton" and resembles the interior structure of an airplane wing. In the skeleton, all the elements of the shape are present, so that the process of construction of the board consist in covering the skeleton. The top and bottom bottom blanks "sandwich" the skeleton and the rails are stacked up using bead and cove strips. The exterior elements are 0.25" with the exception of the nose and tail blocks. The woods used in it's construction include alder, bamboo, poplar, and walnut.
